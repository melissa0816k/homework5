Hong Kong is facing the challenge posed by rapidly aging population. It is appreciated that Hongkongers can have such high life expectancy but we should consider the life quality.

Both female and male have higher life expectancy than before whereas low infant and maternal mortality rates continue. If aging population is growing with low birth rate, it will not only place a heavy financial burden on the whole community, but also aggravate the social structure.




The society get richer but surprisingly fewer babies are born, according to the data.


The birth rate in Hong Kong has dropped signicantly from 35.0 per 1,000 population in 1961 to 8.2 per 1,000 population in 2015. Tracing Hong Kong 's history back to 1970s, we could find that the government worked hard to persuade citizens against having too many childen, for example, doing propaganda "two is enough".
